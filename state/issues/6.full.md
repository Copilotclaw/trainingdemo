Here's what I built from your slide idea:

**`human-rag-exercise.ipynb`** — a 7-step notebook structured around that exact exercise:

1. **The job posting** (translated to English) — imagine you're the paper-only research assistant
2. **Think first** — write your organisational strategy before seeing the data
3. **The mini knowledge base** — 20 real dog & cat articles, printed out so you have to *scan* them
4. **4 customer enquiries** to answer manually, including one that's NOT in the knowledge base (the hallucination trap)
5. **Reflection** — what was hard at 20 articles? Extrapolate to 10,000
6. **The RAG mapping table** — your paper job → system component (corpus, retrieval, embeddings, index, grounding)
7. **Then the machine does it** — FAISS + SentenceTransformers over the same 20 articles, and students compare whether the machine found what they found

The frustration of Enquiry 4 ("diabetic dog food" — not in the KB) is the whole point: that's why grounding/hallucination prevention exists.

I put it first in the README — it's the "start here" before `simple-rag-demo`.
